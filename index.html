About

Mark Levitt

I'm a product manager with twenty years of experience in platform products, APIs, and developer programs at companies including Betfair, Visa Europe, FanDuel, and OpenText.

My background sits across the technical and commercial sides of the role. I've shaped API pricing structures and developer programs from scratch, led platform teams through significant architectural changes, and presented roadmap strategy to CEOs and boards. Along the way I've been involved in several high-profile transactions — Visa Europe's €16.5 billion acquisition, FanDuel's $158 million sale to Paddy Power Betfair, and Betfair's £1.5 billion IPO.

Through Deburson Limited, I take on contract and consulting engagements where there's a specific problem to solve: a platform to define, a team to reorient, or a technology decision that needs rigorous analysis before you commit to it.

Services

What I do

I work with businesses that need a senior product perspective without a permanent headcount commitment. Engagements typically fall into one of these areas.

Platform & API Product Strategy

Defining what a platform should do, how it should be priced, and how it fits into a broader product portfolio. I've built developer programs and API products at Betfair and OpenText — including pricing models, closed beta programs, and the billing infrastructure to support them.

Technology Evaluation & Discovery

Working through build-versus-buy decisions, evaluating platforms on their commercial, technical, and operational merits, and producing a clear recommendation. Useful when you're about to make a significant commitment and want independent analysis before you do.

Product Team Effectiveness

Helping product and engineering teams get unstuck — whether that's improving how work is prioritised, restructuring a roadmap, or moving from infrequent releases to continuous delivery. I've made these transitions at Visa Europe and FanDuel.

Interim & Contract Product Management

Taking on a product management role directly for a defined period — useful during a hiring gap, a product pivot, or when you need an experienced pair of hands on a specific initiative without the overhead of a permanent hire.

Experience

Where I've worked

Twenty years across some of the more interesting moments in digital — from Betfair's IPO to Visa's digital transformation to launching US sports betting after legalisation.

OpenText
2020 — 2023

Principal Product Manager, Cloud Platform

Responsible for foundation services of the OpenText Cloud Platform — a PaaS serving large enterprises and governments worldwide. Led developer pricing and the launch of a developer beta program, designed the billing and reporting system to monetise the program, and championed a shift from monolithic architecture to a modular platform to remove delivery bottlenecks. Also launched a self-service trial capability to reduce the cost of acquiring customers.

FanDuel
2015 — 2019

Product Director, Platform Products

Led the platform product roadmap for a business with 7 million registered users across 43 US states. Played a key role in evaluating sportsbook platform options and planning the launch of FanDuel's Sportsbook after US legalisation — which generated $372 million in turnover in March 2019 alone. Launched a multi-channel notifications platform that improved customer retention by 12% in the first six months. The business was acquired by Paddy Power Betfair for $158 million in 2018.

Visa Europe
2013 — 2015

Area Product Owner, Digital

Led the team delivering V.me by Visa (now Visa Checkout) — an online wallet for card payments at e-commerce retailers. Managed roadmap planning, backlog, release plans, and quality standards across a team of Product Owners. Transitioned the team from a 6-monthly release cycle to continuous delivery, and worked within Visa Europe's regulatory environment across Know Your Customer and anti-money laundering requirements. Visa Europe was acquired by Visa, Inc for €16.5 billion in 2016.

Betfair
2005 — 2013

Technical Product Manager & Developer Advocate

Led a major update to Betfair's Sports Exchange API — used by 70,000+ customers, generating £90 million in revenue, and handling 15 billion data requests and 40 million transactions per week. Delivered a closed beta to select developers, rebuilt the pricing structure to simplify the value proposition, and contributed to Betfair App Cloud (an LXC-based container hosting environment for third-party applications). Ran developer events and spoke at conferences to drive adoption. Betfair completed its IPO at a £1.5 billion valuation in 2010.
